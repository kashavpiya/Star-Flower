Qaulifying Medical Conditions in Illinois

All adult patients 18 years old and over must be diagnosed with one of the following conditions and establish a relationship with the signing doctor. Any adult patient who needs a caregiver can add one. Medical cannabis cards are valid for one, two or three years.

  • Agitation of Alzheimer’s disease
  • HIV/AIDS
  • Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)
  • Arnold-Chiari malformation
  • Cancer
  • Causalgia
  • Chronic inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy
  • Crohn’s disease
  • CRPS (complex regional pain syndrome Type II)
  • Dystonia
  • Fibromyalgia (severe)
  • Fibrous Dysplasia
  • Glaucoma
  • Hepatitis C
  • Hydrocephalus
  • Hydromyelia
  • Interstitial cystitis
  • Lupus
  • Multiple Sclerosis
  • Muscular Dystrophy
  • Myasthenia Gravis
  • Myoclonus
  • Nail-patella syndrome
  • Neurofibromatosis
  • Parkinson’s disease
  • Post-Concussion Syndrome
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Reflex sympathetic dystrophy
  • Residual limb pain
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Seizures
  • Sjogren’s syndrome
  • Spinal cord disease
  • Spinal cord injury
  • Spinocerebellar ataxia
  • Syringomyelia
  • Tarlov cysts
  • Tourette syndrome
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Cachexia/wasting syndrome

Qaulifying Medical Conditions in Iowa

All adult patients 18 years old and over must be diagnosed with one of the following conditions and establish a relationship with the signing doctor. Any adult patient who needs a caregiver can add one. Medical cannabis cards are valid for one year and must be renewed.

  • AIDS or HIV
  • Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)
  • Autism (self-injurious or aggressive behaviors)
  • Cancer
  • Chronic pain
  • Corticobasal Degeneration
  • Crohn’s disease
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Parkinson’s disease
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Seizures
  • Any terminal illness with a probable life expectancy of under one year
  • Ulcerative colitis

Terminal Illness Patients

Illinois residents:

Patients that have been diagnosed with a terminal illness with a probable life expectancy of six (6) months or less may apply for a for six month card. This card only requires one visit and there is no state fee.

Iowa residents:

Patients that have been diagnosed with a terminal illness with a probable life expectancy of 1 year or less may apply.
